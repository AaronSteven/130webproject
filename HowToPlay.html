How to Play Picross!

In this page we will cover some of the history of Picross and some of the basics of playing it!

What is Picross?

Picross is a logic puzzle and a game. Basically, cells are placed on a grid and it is the job of the player to use the row and column numbers to deduce which cells they should click on, and which they should leave blank. It can be great fun if you don't have anything better to be doing.

When was Picross invented?

The history is interesting. In 1987 a Japanese graphics editor named Non Ishida made grid pictures on city skyscrapers that formed when lights were turned on or off. At the same time, Picross was formed as a puzzle by Tetsuya Nishio, a Japanese puzzler. The puzzle began being published in Japanese puzzle magazines and then spread from there. We assume at the rate of at least the common cold.

How to Play Our Picross Game

Our version of Picross is the same as any you have played, other than that you need to read the picorss number columns and rows from right to left for the rows, and bottom to top for the columns. You see, since it was originally a Japanese game, we thought this might be a fun twist and a good homage to the roots of the game if we did that to the rows. Then we figured we better flip the columns too. No half measures.
